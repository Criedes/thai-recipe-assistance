1. Pad Thai
Ingredients:

200g rice noodles
2 tbsp vegetable oil
200g shrimp or chicken
2 eggs
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 cup bean sprouts
2 spring onions, chopped
1/4 cup crushed peanuts
Lime wedges
Pad Thai Sauce:

3 tbsp fish sauce
3 tbsp tamarind paste
2 tbsp palm sugar
Instructions:

Soak rice noodles in warm water for 30 minutes.
Heat oil in a wok, add garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant.
Add shrimp or chicken, and cook until done.
Push the meat to one side, scramble the eggs on the other side, and mix everything together.
Add noodles and Pad Thai sauce, stir-fry until noodles are soft.
Mix in bean sprouts and spring onions.
Serve with crushed peanuts and lime wedges.
2. Tom Yum Goong (Spicy Shrimp Soup)
Ingredients:

500g shrimp
3 cups water or chicken stock
1 cup mushrooms
2 stalks lemongrass
3 kaffir lime leaves
3-4 bird's eye chilies
1-inch piece galangal
3 tbsp fish sauce
2 tbsp lime juice
1 tsp sugar
Fresh coriander
Instructions:

Bring water or stock to a boil, add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, and chilies.
Add mushrooms and shrimp, cook until shrimp are done.
Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar.
Garnish with fresh coriander.
3. Green Curry (Gaeng Keow Wan)
Ingredients:

400ml coconut milk
2 tbsp green curry paste
300g chicken, sliced
1 cup eggplant, chopped
1 cup bamboo shoots
3 kaffir lime leaves
1 red chili, sliced
1 cup Thai basil leaves
2 tbsp fish sauce
1 tbsp palm sugar
Instructions:

Heat a spoonful of coconut milk in a pot, add green curry paste, and cook until fragrant.
Add chicken and stir until cooked.
Pour in the remaining coconut milk, bring to a boil.
Add eggplant, bamboo shoots, kaffir lime leaves, and chili.
Season with fish sauce and palm sugar.
Simmer until vegetables are tender, then stir in basil leaves before serving.
4. Massaman Curry
Ingredients:

400ml coconut milk
2 tbsp massaman curry paste
500g beef or chicken
2 potatoes, diced
1 onion, chopped
1/4 cup roasted peanuts
2 tbsp fish sauce
2 tbsp tamarind paste
2 tbsp palm sugar
1 cinnamon stick
3 cardamom pods
Instructions:

Heat a spoonful of coconut milk in a pot, add curry paste, and cook until fragrant.
Add beef or chicken and cook until browned.
Add the remaining coconut milk, potatoes, onions, peanuts, cinnamon stick, and cardamom pods.
Season with fish sauce, tamarind paste, and palm sugar.
Simmer until meat and potatoes are tender.
5. Som Tum (Papaya Salad)
Ingredients:

2 cups shredded green papaya
1 carrot, shredded
1 tomato, sliced
3 cloves garlic
2-3 bird's eye chilies
2 tbsp fish sauce
1 tbsp palm sugar
2 tbsp lime juice
1/4 cup peanuts
1/4 cup dried shrimp (optional)
Long beans, cut into pieces
Instructions:

In a mortar, pound garlic and chilies until bruised.
Add shredded papaya, carrot, tomato, long beans, and dried shrimp.
Season with fish sauce, palm sugar, and lime juice.
Toss everything together, garnish with peanuts.
6. Moo Ping (Grilled Pork Skewers)
Ingredients:

500g pork shoulder, thinly sliced
3 tbsp soy sauce
3 tbsp fish sauce
2 tbsp oyster sauce
2 tbsp sugar
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 tsp white pepper
Bamboo skewers
Instructions:

Marinate pork with soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, garlic, and white pepper for at least 2 hours.
Thread pork onto skewers.
Grill over medium heat until cooked and slightly charred.
7. Tom Kha Gai (Chicken in Coconut Soup)
Ingredients:

400ml coconut milk
300g chicken breast, sliced
2 cups chicken stock
1-inch piece galangal, sliced
2 stalks lemongrass
3 kaffir lime leaves
200g mushrooms
2-3 bird's eye chilies
3 tbsp fish sauce
2 tbsp lime juice
Fresh coriander
Instructions:

Bring chicken stock to a boil, add galangal, lemongrass, and kaffir lime leaves.
Add chicken and mushrooms, cook until chicken is done.
Pour in coconut milk, and bring to a gentle boil.
Season with fish sauce and lime juice.
Garnish with fresh coriander.
8. Larb Gai (Spicy Chicken Salad)
Ingredients:

300g ground chicken
2 tbsp fish sauce
1 tbsp lime juice
1 tbsp roasted rice powder
2 shallots, sliced
2 spring onions, chopped
Fresh mint leaves
Fresh coriander
Bird's eye chilies, chopped
Instructions:

Cook ground chicken in a pan until done.
In a bowl, mix cooked chicken with fish sauce, lime juice, and roasted rice powder.
Add shallots, spring onions, mint leaves, and coriander.
Mix well and serve with fresh vegetables.
9. Pad Kra Pao (Basil Stir-Fry)
Ingredients:

300g chicken or pork, minced
3 cloves garlic, minced
2-3 bird's eye chilies, chopped
2 tbsp soy sauce
2 tbsp fish sauce
1 tsp sugar
1 cup Thai basil leaves
1 egg (optional)
Instructions:

Heat oil in a wok, add garlic and chilies, and stir-fry until fragrant.
Add minced meat and cook until done.
Season with soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar.
Stir in Thai basil leaves and cook until wilted.
Serve with rice, and top with a fried egg if desired.
10. Mango Sticky Rice
Ingredients:

1 cup glutinous rice
1 cup coconut milk
1/4 cup sugar
1/2 tsp salt
2 ripe mangoes, sliced
Sesame seeds (optional)
Instructions:

Soak glutinous rice in water for at least 4 hours, then steam until cooked.
In a saucepan, heat coconut milk, sugar, and salt until sugar dissolves.
Mix the cooked rice with coconut milk mixture, and let sit for 20 minutes.
Serve the sticky rice with sliced mangoes and sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired.
11. Panang Curry
Ingredients:

400ml coconut milk
2 tbsp Panang curry paste
300g beef, sliced
1 red chili, sliced
2 kaffir lime leaves, finely sliced
1 tbsp fish sauce
1 tbsp palm sugar
1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
1 cup Thai basil leaves
Instructions:

Heat a spoonful of coconut milk in a pot, add Panang curry paste, and cook until fragrant.
Add beef and cook until browned.
Pour in the remaining coconut milk, bring to a boil.
Add chili, kaffir lime leaves, fish sauce, and palm sugar.
Simmer until beef is tender, then stir in roasted peanuts and basil leaves before serving.
12. Khao Pad (Thai Fried Rice)
Ingredients:

2 cups cooked jasmine rice
200g chicken or shrimp
2 eggs
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 onion, chopped
1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
2 tbsp soy sauce
2 tbsp fish sauce
1 tsp sugar
Spring onions, chopped
Lime wedges
Instructions:

Heat oil in a wok, add garlic and onion, and stir-fry until fragrant.
Add chicken or shrimp, and cook until done.
Push the meat to one side, scramble the eggs on the other side, and mix everything together.
Add mixed vegetables and rice, stir-fry until heated through.
Season with soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar.
Garnish with spring onions and serve with lime wedges.
13. Yum Nua (Thai Beef Salad)
Ingredients:

300g beef steak
1 cucumber, sliced
1 tomato, sliced
1 red onion, sliced
2-3 bird's eye chilies, chopped
Fresh mint leaves
Fresh coriander
Dressing:

3 tbsp fish sauce
2 tbsp lime juice
1 tbsp sugar
Instructions:

Grill the beef steak to desired doneness, let it rest, and then slice thinly.
In a bowl, combine cucumber, tomato, red onion, chilies, mint leaves, and coriander.
Add beef slices to the bowl.
Mix the dressing ingredients, pour over the salad, and toss well before serving.
14. Tod Mun Pla (Thai Fish Cakes)
Ingredients:

500g white fish fillets
2 tbsp red curry paste
1 egg
1 tbsp fish sauce
1 tbsp sugar
2 kaffir lime leaves, finely sliced
1 cup green beans, chopped
Oil for frying
Instructions:

In a food processor, blend fish fillets, red curry paste, egg, fish sauce, and sugar until smooth.
Transfer the mixture to a bowl, and stir in kaffir lime leaves and green beans.
Form the mixture into small patties.
Heat oil in a pan, and fry the fish cakes until golden brown and cooked through.
15. Gaeng Daeng (Red Curry)
Ingredients:

400ml coconut milk
2 tbsp red curry paste
300g chicken, sliced
1 cup eggplant, chopped
1 red chili, sliced
3 kaffir lime leaves
1 cup Thai basil leaves
2 tbsp fish sauce
1 tbsp palm sugar
Instructions:

Heat a spoonful of coconut milk in a pot, add red curry paste, and cook until fragrant.
Add chicken and cook until browned.
Pour in the remaining coconut milk, bring to a boil.
Add eggplant, red chili, and kaffir lime leaves.
Season with fish sauce and palm sugar.
Simmer until vegetables are tender, then stir in basil leaves before serving.
16. Khao Soi (Northern Thai Noodle Soup)
Ingredients:

400ml coconut milk
2 tbsp Khao Soi curry paste
300g chicken thighs
2 cups chicken stock
1 cup egg noodles
1 red onion, sliced
1 lime, cut into wedges
Pickled mustard greens (optional)
Fresh coriander
Instructions:

Heat a spoonful of coconut milk in a pot, add Khao Soi curry paste, and cook until fragrant.
Add chicken thighs and cook until browned.
Pour in the remaining coconut milk and chicken stock, bring to a boil.
Simmer until chicken is tender.
Cook egg noodles according to package instructions.
Serve the curry over noodles, garnished with red onion, lime wedges, pickled mustard greens, and fresh coriander.
17. Gai Tod (Thai Fried Chicken)
Ingredients:

500g chicken wings or drumsticks
3 tbsp fish sauce
2 tbsp soy sauce
1 tbsp sugar
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 tsp white pepper
Oil for frying
Instructions:

Marinate chicken with fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, garlic, and white pepper for at least 2 hours.
Heat oil in a deep pan.
Fry the chicken until golden brown and cooked through.
Serve with sweet chili sauce.
18. Pla Rad Prik (Fried Fish with Chili Sauce)
Ingredients:

1 whole fish (such as tilapia or snapper), cleaned
3 tbsp fish sauce
2 tbsp lime juice
2 tbsp palm sugar
3-4 bird's eye chilies, chopped
3 cloves garlic, minced
Fresh coriander
Instructions:

Score the fish on both sides and fry until crispy and cooked through.
In a bowl, mix fish sauce, lime juice, palm sugar, chilies, and garlic.
Pour the sauce over the fried fish.
Garnish with fresh coriander before serving.
19. Khanom Jeen Nam Ya (Rice Noodles with Fish Curry)
Ingredients:

400ml coconut milk
2 tbsp red curry paste
300g fish fillets
2 cups chicken stock
200g rice noodles
Fresh vegetables (bean sprouts, cucumber, cabbage)
Instructions:

Cook fish in chicken stock until done, then blend into a smooth paste.
Heat a spoonful of coconut milk in a pot, add red curry paste, and cook until fragrant.
Add fish paste and remaining coconut milk.
Simmer for 10 minutes.
Cook rice noodles according to package instructions.
Serve curry over rice noodles with fresh vegetables.
20. Kai Jeow (Thai Omelette)
Ingredients:

3 eggs
2 tbsp fish sauce
1/4 cup minced pork (optional)
1 spring onion, chopped
Oil for frying
Instructions:

Beat eggs with fish sauce and minced pork.
Heat oil in a pan, pour in the egg mixture.
Cook until golden and crispy.
Garnish with chopped spring onion before serving.
